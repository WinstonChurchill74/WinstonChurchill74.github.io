Winston Churchill's Political Career

In 1900, Winston Churchill joined the British Conservative Party and was elected to Parliament.

In 1904, Churchill joined the British Liberal Party.

In 1910, He became the Secretary of State for the Home Department, a position that dealt with the police, prisons, and the state of criminal law.

In 1911, He became the First Lord of the Admiralty. While he served this position, he prepared the UK for what he thought was going to be a large war, developed a well trained war staff, re-modernized the Navy, and helped develop the tank.

In 1917, He became the Minister of Munitions. He was responsible for supplying the troops on the front line with ammunition, a very important role during WWI.

In 1924, Churchill was elected to the Chancellor of the Exchequer, a position in which you are the head of Her Majesty's Treasury, which is only one office down from Prime Minister.

Finally in 1940 after Neville Chamberlain resigned, Winston Churchill became Great Britain's Prime Minister, the head of government and leader of the majority party in Parliament (Coalition at the time).

In May of 1945, Churchill desolved the coalition government and became Conservative. He was later not elected in July of 1945.

Churchill was reelected as Prime Minister (Conservative again) in October of 1951. He lead Great Britain through a lot of the Cold War and then resigned in April of 1955.
